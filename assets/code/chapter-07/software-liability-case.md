# AutoPilot Express Release Evidence

**Status:** Fictional instructional scenario

## Decision

TransitTech must choose whether to launch, limit rain use, or delay an
autonomous bus release after a hard-braking defect appears in tests.

## Test Evidence

* The pilot completed 50,000 trips with no grave injury.
* Sixty of 20,000 simulated heavy-rain runs caused an abrupt stop.
* Ten runs, 0.05% of all heavy-rain runs, reached a force that may injure a standing rider.
* The test does not prove rider exposure or how well the simulation matches road use.
* Dry and light-rain tests did not show the flaw.

## Choice Evidence

* A full fix may delay launch by 8-12 months.
* Added work and lost revenue may total $9.2 million.
* The rain limit depends on an official warning and trained drivers.
* Fast storms may start before a warning.
* The contract does not assign extra-driver costs or give the transit agency a right to review safety tests.

## Student Task

Use the evidence with the chapter's named ethical frameworks, laws,
policies, and stakeholder tests. State any assumption you add. Do not
treat a missing fact as proof.
