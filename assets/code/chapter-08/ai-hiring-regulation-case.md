# TalentBridge Launch Evidence

**Status:** Fictional instructional scenario

## Decision

TalentBridge AI must choose whether to launch, run a controlled pilot,
or delay an AI hiring platform in France and Germany.

## Model Evidence

* The platform ranks resumes and scores recorded interviews.
* An outside 400-resume test found an eight-point average score gap tied to school type.
* TalentBridge disputes the test but has not published its own group results.
* Video scores use speech pace, eye movement, word choice, and a "confidence" measure.
* The contract does not state whether the tool infers emotion or uses biometric identification.

## Worker Evidence

* TalentBridge plans to move 45 Phoenix data-labeling jobs to a vendor in the Philippines.
* The US team received 90 days' notice but no severance, training, or placement help.
* The vendor contract names pay and hours but gives TalentBridge no audit right for worker complaints.
* Twelve engineers hold H-1B status through TalentBridge.
* Two engineers reported bias concerns and want an independent report path.

## Student Task

Use the evidence with the chapter's named ethical frameworks, laws,
policies, and stakeholder tests. State any assumption you add. Do not
treat a missing fact as proof.
