# MedPredict Contract Evidence

**Status:** Fictional instructional scenario

## Decision

Valley Regional Medical Center must choose whether to sign, negotiate,
or decline a three-year, $1.8 million AI sepsis contract.

## Performance Evidence

* Vendor trials report 82% sensitivity and 95% specificity.
* Twelve academic hospitals supplied the training and test data.
* The vendor has not supplied subgroup results or a local validation.
* The training sites served more White and privately insured patients than the local hospital.
* The local hospital serves a diverse urban community, and 15% of its patients are uninsured or covered by Medicaid.
* Five percent of patients without sepsis received a false alert under the vendor's test conditions.

## Data and Contract Evidence

* The workflow sends vital signs, lab results, and EHR data to a vendor cloud.
* The proposed contract includes a business associate agreement.
* Approved subcontractors may process data, but the main contract does not list them.
* Scores show a risk label but do not show the main factors behind it.
* Nurses already manage many alerts in the EHR.
* The vendor may update the model each quarter with 14 days' notice.
* The hospital receives no independent model-audit right.

## Student Task

Use the evidence with the chapter's named ethical frameworks, laws,
policies, and stakeholder tests. State any assumption you add. Do not
treat a missing fact as proof.
